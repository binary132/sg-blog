RVM on Gentoo

24/Jun 2015
Development Ruby Rails Gentoo

RVM is bad and Gentoo is good, and other blasphemies.

Rails

Today I decided to expand my repertoire a bit with Rails. I’m mostly focused on backend nuts and bolts, but I need a full-stack framework under my belt, and Ruby on Rails is arguably the best option due to its simplicity and ease to get off the ground. It’s obviously very popular – I’ve had lots of people insist I explore it – so I’m finally getting around to it.

I’ve always avoided fullstack work in favor of API + frontend. What can I say? I’m a control freak. I adore simple, explicit interfaces and precise expectations. The last time I explored Rails, it seemed like a huge, impenetrable monstrosity. But it continues to be one of the most well-known and useful tools in the free world, so learn it I shall.

Gentoo

However, I discovered that when I chose to use moon stone on my workstation, I overlooked a few details of its evolution. You see, Gentoo likes to manage packages GENTOO’S way. Mixing package managers usually isn’t an issue on other systems – most people know better than to use apt-get to install Rails on an LTS distribution – but Gentoo is highly opinionated about package and dependence versioning, so most Gentoo users would use ‘eselect’ to choose their version of Ruby.
